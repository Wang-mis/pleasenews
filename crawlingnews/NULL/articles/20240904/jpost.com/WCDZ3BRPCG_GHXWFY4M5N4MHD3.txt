What’s next for IDF leadership?: Ground Forces Command chief Tamir Yadai resigns
AMIR BOHBOT
SEPTEMBER 3, 2024 11:15
20240903
https://www.jpost.com/israel-news/defense-news/article-817513
Maj.-Gen. Tamir Yadai, the head of the IDF’s Ground Forces Command, is to resign from his position for personal reasons, Walla reported on Tuesday.Yadai informed Chief of Staff Lt.-Gen. Herzi Halevi of his intention to resign in the coming weeks. Defense Minister Yoav Gallant has approved the request.The IDF’s General Staff has been on high alert recently due to the war in Gaza, discussions about the prisoner exchange deal, and other anticipated changes within the General Staff.  Yadai was considered a future candidate for deputy chief of staff. However, according to assessments, current deputy chief Maj.-Gen. Amir Baram is not expected to finish his term soon.In light of Yadai’s resignation, Halevi will appoint a new Ground Forces Command chief, increasing the likelihood that Commander of the Northern Command Maj.-Gen. Ori Gordin, will be appointed next Deputy Chief of Staff. Major-General Tamir Yadai and other senior Israeli officials. 2011. (credit: Ariel Hermoni/Flickr)It is important to note that there has been criticism of Gordin’s conduct recently as Northern Command Commander during the conflict with Hezbollah.Yadai's history in the IDFYadai, a highly respected officer in the defense establishment, began his IDF service in 1988 as a fighter in the 51st Battalion and has since risen through the ranks. He commanded the 13th Battalion and the Egoz Unit, became the Golani Brigade commander at a young age, and emerged as one of the leading field commanders.After the Second Lebanon War, he was given command of the Edom Formation. Since then, he has commanded the Judea and Samaria Division, the Home Front Command, Central Command, and finally, in his last role, the Ground Forces. He is regarded as one of the brightest minds in the General Staff.At the beginning of the war, Yadai was among the generals who pushed for ground maneuvers and expressed confidence in the reserve forces.
